Chipotle Tofu Chilaquiles

Description

This recipe is a twist on classic chilaquiles! Make your own chipotle sauce and pour it over some tortilla chips, then crumble tofu on top for a delicious vegan version of this amazing Mexican dish.

Ingredients

For the chilaquiles:

  • 5 small white yellow corn tortillas
  • 1/2 large white onion (diced)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 15-ounce can crushed tomatoes or tomato sauce
  • 1 chipotle pepper in adobo sauce
  • 1 Tbsp adobo sauce
  • 1/2 cup veggie stock

For the tofu:

  • 8 ounces extra-firm tofu
  • 1/2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/4 tsp chili powder
  • 1/4 tsp sea salt

Instructions

  1. Press/drain tofu and preheat oven to 175 degrees C
  2. (If baking your own tortillas) Brush or spray both sides of yoru tortillas with olive or avocado oil and lightly dust with sea salt. Stack nad cut into triangles, and arrange in a single layer on a large baking sheet. Bake for 10-12 minutes, flipping once halfway through, until crisp and slightly golden.
  3. While chips are baking, heat a large skillet over medium heat and prep onion and garlic
  4. Once hot, add 1 Tbsp olive or avocado oil and onion. Cook, stirring frequently, until soft and slightly browned (~3 minutes). Then add garlic and cook for 1-2 minutes more
  5. Add tomato sauce, diced chipotle and adobo sauce, and veggie stock. Heat until bubbly. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 5 minutes.
  6. Transfer sauce to a blender (optional). Set aside
  7. Use a fork to crumble the tofu and place skillet back over medium heat
  8. Add a bit of oil to the pan and add tofu. Let lightly brown for 3-4 minutes, stirring once or twice. Add seasonings (chili powder, salt, garlic powder, and cumin) and stir. Cook for another 2 minutees, then remove from pan and set aside
  9. Add chips to the pan and pour over sauce, stirring quickly to coat. Then top with tofu scramble, fresh onion, and cilantro, and serve immediately.
